Abstract

Preference-based reinforcement learning (PbRL) has emerged as a promising paradigm for learning robot behaviors aligned with human intent. However, its scalability is fundamentally limited by the need to collect expensive human preference labels for every new task. In this work, we present \textbf{PrefO2O}, an offline-to-online latent preference transfer framework that enables zero-shot PbRL on unseen target tasks without requiring any human feedback. Our core idea is to learn a task-invariant latent preference space from offline preference data on source tasks, where abstract and generalizable preference representations are disentangled from task-specific variations. This learned manifold allows PrefO2O to transfer preference structures and reward priors to new tasks robustly under distribution shift, with confidence-weighted optimal transport alignment generating reliable pseudo-preference supervision for online PbRL. Extensive experiments on robotic manipulation and locomotion benchmarks demonstrate that PrefO2O substantially outperforms existing preference-transfer baselines and matches or exceeds PbRL methods trained with real human feedback, while generalizing across multi-to-one and one-to-one transfer settings as well as across different robot embodiments.
